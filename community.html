Games For Change (G4C)

Games for Change (G4C) chapters serve as local ambassadors for G4C's mission, promoting social impact through regular events, community engagement, educational initiatives, and advocacy campaigns. These chapters play a crucial role in connecting members with like-minded individuals and organizations in their communities, fostering collaboration and driving positive change. Additionally, G4C chapters provide opportunities for members to engage with a global network of chapters, special interest groups (SIGs), and committees, enabling them to contribute to the broader movement for using games as a force for good on a global scale.

Special Interest Groups

Games for Change (G4C) Special Interest Groups (SIGs) are worldwide communities led by dedicated volunteers. Each SIG caters to individuals of all backgrounds and expertise levels, spanning from students to professionals, independent developers to industry giants. These SIGs focus on specific topics, issues, resources, or affinity groups within the realm of game development and social impact. They provide a platform for members to share knowledge, collaborate on projects, and advocate for positive change in their respective areas of interest within the gaming community.

Global Mentorship Program

Crafted to empower gaming professionals worldwide, the Games for Change Global Mentorship Program offers a platform for members to uplift each other and advance their careers. This initiative facilitates mentees in receiving guidance from experienced individuals within the gaming industry, while mentors have the opportunity to hone their mentorship skills and cultivate meaningful connections. Through this program, participants not only gain valuable insights and support but also contribute to the growth and development of the gaming community as a whole.
