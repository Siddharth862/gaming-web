Global Gaming Initiative

Games for Change is an organization that promotes the use of games and immersive media for social impact. They support game developers, host events and conferences, and advocate for games as a tool for education, healthcare, and social justice.

Join Us

Mission and goals

Games for Change (G4C) is dedicated to harnessing the transformative power of games and immersive media to address pressing social challenges and catalyze positive change in communities worldwide. Our mission is to empower game creators, educators, policymakers, and innovators to leverage the unique capabilities of games for education, healthcare, and social justice.

At the core of our work is the belief that games are more than entertainment—they are powerful tools for fostering empathy, promoting learning, and driving social impact. Through our initiatives, programs, and collaborations, we strive to build a global community of changemakers who are passionate about using games as a force for good.

Our ultimate goal is to create a world where games are recognized as a legitimate and impactful medium for addressing complex social issues, driving meaningful engagement, and inspiring positive action. We envision a future where game developers, educators, and policymakers work hand-in-hand to harness the full potential of games to tackle some of the most pressing challenges facing our society.

With a focus on innovation, collaboration, and inclusivity, G4C continues to push the boundaries of what games can achieve and inspire a new generation of creators and players to join us in the journey towards a more equitable and compassionate world.

our community

Games4Change (G4C)

Games for Change (G4C) chapters serve as local ambassadors for G4C's mission, promoting social impact through regular events, community engagement, educational initiatives, and advocacy campaigns. These chapters play a crucial role in connecting members with like-minded individuals and organizations in their communities, fostering collaboration and driving positive change. Additionally, G4C chapters provide opportunities for members to engage with a global network of chapters, special interest groups (SIGs), and committees, enabling them to contribute to the broader movement for using games as a force for good on a global scale.

Special Interest Groups

Games for Change (G4C) Special Interest Groups (SIGs) are worldwide communities led by dedicated volunteers. Each SIG caters to individuals of all backgrounds and expertise levels, spanning from students to professionals, independent developers to industry giants. These SIGs focus on specific topics, issues, resources, or affinity groups within the realm of game development and social impact. They provide a platform for members to share knowledge, collaborate on projects, and advocate for positive change in their respective areas of interest within the gaming community.

Global Mentorship Program

Crafted to empower gaming professionals worldwide, the Games for Change Global Mentorship Program offers a platform for members to uplift each other and advance their careers. This initiative facilitates mentees in receiving guidance from experienced individuals within the gaming industry, while mentors have the opportunity to hone their mentorship skills and cultivate meaningful connections. Through this program, participants not only gain valuable insights and support but also contribute to the growth and development of the gaming community as a whole.

NEWS

A 13-year-old boy said he is the first person to beat the original Tetris and reach the game's kill screen. The teen, who goes by Blue Scuti on social media, posted a video Tuesday on his YouTube page showing him appearing to defeat the popular puzzle game. The record was previously held by an artificial intelligence bot, according to 404 Media. Blue Scuti was about 38 minutes into playing the game when he realized he was close to beating it, the video shows.

By Minyvonne Burke

Apple has reversed course under regulatory pressure and cleared the way for a nettlesome adversary, video game maker Epic Games, to set up an alternative store for iPhone apps in Europe. The about-face disclosed Friday is the latest twist in a bitter fight between Apple and Epic Games, the maker of the popular Fortnite video game, over the way iPhone apps are distributed and the fees for digital transactions that occur within them. Apple attributed the change of heart to reassurances from Epic that it won’t violate its requirements for getting access to iPhone owners. Epic had brazenly broke the rules in the U.S. in 2020 to trigger an antitrust lawsuit alleging Apple’s App Store is a monopoly.

By The Associated Press

Electronic Arts announced Wednesday that it will cut 5% of its workforce, part of a plan that includes reducing office space and ending work on some video games. EA employed 13,400 workers as of the end of March 2023, according to its most recent annual filing with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission in May. That means the layoffs may affect about 670 jobs. The company’s announcement marks the latest headcount reduction among video game developers in recent months, continuing a broader trend of significant downsizing across the tech industry.
